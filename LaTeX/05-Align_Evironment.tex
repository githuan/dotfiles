Solve the equation $0 = 3x^3+3x^2-6x$.

\begin{align}
	0 &= 3x^3+3x^2-6x \\
	&= 3(x^3 +x^2-2x) \\
	&= 3x(x2^2+x-2) \\
	&= 3x(x+2) (x-1) \\
	& \therefore \\
	x &= 0, -2, 1
\end{align}

Put star sign "\textbf*" in \{align\textbf*\} option will not show the steps:

\begin{align*}
	0 &= 3x^3+3x^2-6x \\
	&= 3(x^3 +x^2-2x) \\
	&= 3x(x2^2+x-2) \\
	&= 3x(x+2) (x-1) \\
	& \therefore \\
	x &= 0, -2, 1
\end{align*}
